Scoring Dimensions

Strategic Fit (20%)

Whether the brand treats AI assistants as a strategic channel and wants to protect and grow visibility in LLM-generated answers.

Technical Fit (20%)

Content footprint, web stack (EDS/AEM ideal), and ability to act on optimisation recommendations.

Functional Coverage (20%)

Overlap with LLM Optimizer capabilities — AI visibility monitoring, share-of-voice, citation tracking, and content recommendations.

Commercial Viability (15%)

Brand size, category competitiveness, and innovation budget for an emerging capability.

Competitive Position (15%)

GEO/AEO startups in play (Profound, Peec, Otterly) and Adobe's advantage via the content and optimisation stack.

Delivery Risk (10%)

Organisational readiness to act on AI-era content guidance and accept non-deterministic outcomes.

Deployment Models

SaaS (works with any web content)

Cloud service that monitors and optimises brand presence across AI assistants regardless of CMS.

Best with EDS / AEM + Sites Optimizer

Tightest loop when content is on Adobe's stack — recommendations flow into authoring and optimisation.

Products Covered

Adobe LLM Optimizer

Monitor and improve how a brand appears in AI assistants and LLM-generated answers.

AI visibility & share-of-voice

Track brand presence and citations across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and others.

Content recommendations

Guidance to make content more citable and answer-ready for LLMs.

Agentic optimisation

Automated detection and suggested actions to improve AI answer presence.

Key Signals

Concern about AI / LLM visibility

Brand worried about being absent or misrepresented in AI answers — core LLM Optimizer value.

Organic traffic shifting to AI answers

Declining click-through as users get answers from assistants — GEO/AEO urgency.

Competitive, high-consideration category

Travel, finance, retail, tech, or healthcare where AI answers influence buyers.

Content-rich brand

A large content footprint to monitor and optimise.

Existing AEM / EDS / Sites Optimizer

Adobe content stack in place — a tight optimisation loop.

Forward-looking digital team

Budget and appetite for emerging GenAI capabilities.

Brand / reputation sensitivity

Wants to control how AI describes the brand.

Red Flags

No SEO / content maturity

Lacks the content foundation to optimise for AI answers.

GenAI skepticism / no budget

No appetite to invest in an emerging, fast-evolving capability.

Negligible LLM exposure

Niche B2B where AI-assistant traffic is immaterial.

Wants guaranteed rankings

LLM outputs are non-deterministic — set expectations or disqualify.

Competitive Alternatives

Profound

Leading GEO/AEO startup for AI answer analytics. Adobe wins by closing the loop into content and optimisation.

Peec AI / Otterly.AI

AI-visibility monitoring tools. Adobe wins on integration with the content supply chain.

BrightEdge / Conductor (AI features)

SEO incumbents adding AI tracking. Adobe wins on native authoring plus agentic action.

Do nothing / wait

Many brands are undecided — the risk is competitors building AI visibility first.
