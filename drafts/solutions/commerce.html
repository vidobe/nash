Scoring Dimensions

Strategic Fit (20%)

How central commerce is to the customer's digital revenue strategy, and whether Adobe Commerce supports their B2B, B2C, or D2C ambitions and omnichannel roadmap.

Technical Fit (20%)

Existing commerce platform, deployment preference (Cloud PaaS, ACCS SaaS, on-prem, or EDS storefront), catalog complexity, and integration landscape (ERP, OMS, PIM, payments).

Functional Coverage (20%)

Overlap between requirements and native capability — B2B, multi-store, Catalog Service, Live Search, Product Recommendations, Payment Services.

Commercial Viability (15%)

GMV, order volume, and whether the deal is sized for Adobe Commerce licensing versus a lighter SaaS platform.

Competitive Position (15%)

Which platforms are in play (Shopify Plus, SFCC, commercetools, SAP, BigCommerce, VTEX) and Adobe's differentiation via the Experience Cloud.

Delivery Risk (10%)

Migration and catalog/data scope, integration count, partner availability, and replatforming timeline.

Deployment Models

Adobe Commerce on Cloud (PaaS)

Adobe-hosted managed cloud on dedicated infrastructure. The established enterprise model with full control and extensibility.

Adobe Commerce as a Cloud Service (ACCS)

SaaS-native, API-first, composable commerce that auto-updates. Fits customers wanting SaaS simplicity and a headless architecture.

Adobe Commerce (on-premise)

Self-hosted for strict control or data-residency requirements. Heaviest operational burden — position the path to cloud.

Edge Delivery Services storefront

Composable storefront on EDS with commerce drop-ins — sub-second performance and Git-based authoring on top of any Commerce backend.

Products Covered

Adobe Commerce

Core B2C and B2B commerce platform — catalog, cart, checkout, promotions, and merchandising.

Commerce as a Cloud Service

SaaS-native, API-first commerce services for composable architectures.

Catalog Service & Live Search

SaaS-powered catalog, search, and browse with AI-driven relevancy.

Product Recommendations

Sensei-powered AI product recommendations across the storefront.

B2B for Adobe Commerce

Company accounts, shared catalogs, negotiated quotes, requisition lists, and purchase approvals.

Payment Services

Native payments, fraud protection, and order/payment reconciliation.

Key Signals

B2B commerce requirements

Company accounts, shared catalogs, negotiated quotes, requisition lists, or purchase approvals — a core Adobe Commerce strength.

Large or complex catalog

Tens of thousands of SKUs, complex configurable/bundled products, or frequent catalog change — Catalog Service + Live Search.

Multi-store / multi-brand / multi-locale

Multiple storefronts, brands, currencies, or markets from one platform — Adobe Commerce multi-store architecture.

Headless / composable commerce

API-first, MACH, or custom front end — ACCS or an EDS storefront with commerce drop-ins.

Existing Magento footprint

Running Magento Open Source or Adobe Commerce (1.x/2.x) — migration or upgrade play; identify version and customizations.

High order volume / peak traffic

Flash sales, seasonal spikes, or high transaction throughput — cloud auto-scaling argument.

Storefront performance priority

Poor Core Web Vitals or conversion lost to slow pages — Edge Delivery Services storefront.

ERP / OMS / PIM integration

Deep back-office integration (SAP, NetSuite, Akeneo) — factor into delivery; App Builder / API Mesh.

Personalisation & recommendations

AI product recommendations and targeted merchandising — Product Recommendations + Target.

Subscription / recurring commerce

Subscriptions, replenishment, or recurring billing — assess via partner extensions.

Marketplace / multi-vendor

Third-party seller marketplace — assess via partner solutions and flag complexity.

Global tax / cross-border

Multi-currency, multi-tax, and cross-border compliance — native plus partner (Vertex, Avalara).

Adobe stack present

AEM, Analytics, Target, or RT-CDP in use — a unified Experience Cloud commerce story.

Payment complexity

Multiple PSPs, fraud, or reconciliation needs — Payment Services.

Red Flags

Very small catalog / low GMV

A handful of SKUs and modest revenue — a Shopify-tier solution likely wins on cost and speed.

Simple D2C with no complexity

Standard B2C with no B2B, multi-store, or integration needs — a lighter SaaS platform may fit better.

No commerce operations team

No merchandising or commerce-ops capability — adoption and run risk.

Replatform fatigue / no budget

Recently replatformed or no appetite for a multi-month project — timing is wrong.

Pure marketplace play

Core need is a multi-vendor marketplace with little Adobe ecosystem fit — qualify carefully.

Competitive Alternatives

Shopify Plus

Wins on speed-to-market and cost for B2C. Adobe wins on B2B depth, extensibility, and Experience Cloud integration.

Salesforce Commerce Cloud

Strong for Salesforce-centric retailers. Adobe wins on B2B, content via AEM, and cost flexibility.

commercetools

Composable/MACH leader. Adobe wins with ACCS composability plus an integrated experience and content stack.

SAP Commerce (Hybris)

Strong in complex B2B/ERP-tied deals. Adobe wins on time-to-value, UX, and marketing integration.

BigCommerce

SaaS simplicity for mid-market. Adobe wins on enterprise B2B, scale, and ecosystem.

VTEX

Unified commerce and marketplace. Adobe wins on Experience Cloud and content-led commerce.
